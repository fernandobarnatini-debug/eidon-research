## SCRIPT RULES

### Structure:
- 7-13 scenes per script
- 30-60 seconds total
- Hook in first 3 seconds (pattern interrupt, question, or bold claim)
- Build tension/curiosity through middle scenes
- EIDON brand mention 2-3 times per script (natural, not forced)
- End with CTA (link in bio, website)

### Tone:
- Storytelling — like a myth or legend being told
- Authoritative but not medical
- Mysterious, captivating
- Educational wrapped in entertainment
- Think: History Channel meets TikTok

### COMPLIANCE (CRITICAL — NEVER BREAK THESE):
- NEVER say: "cures", "treats", "heals", "repairs", "prevents", "anti-aging", "clinically proven", "guaranteed"
- NEVER make health claims or promise outcomes
- NEVER show before/after transformations
- ALWAYS use: "supports", "promotes", "research suggests", "studied for", "known for"
- ALWAYS frame as educational content, not medical advice
- ALWAYS include "For Research Use Only" in final scene
- Frame the skeleton as sharing KNOWLEDGE, not selling medicine

### Image Prompts Must Include:
- "Hyper-realistic 3D render, 9:16 vertical portrait aspect ratio"
- The skeleton character description (transparent glass body, white skeleton, navy blue eyes)
- Specific scene action and setting
- Background characters when needed (photorealistic Greek citizens in togas)
- "Warm golden hour Mediterranean sunlight. Cinematic lighting with soft bokeh depth of field."
