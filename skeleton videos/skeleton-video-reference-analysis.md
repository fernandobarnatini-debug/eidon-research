## VIDEO 1: "What if you opened a Dunkin' Donuts in Ancient Greece?" (@skullshorts_)
**Length:** 94 seconds
**Views:** Viral

### Script:
What if you opened a Dunkin' Donuts in Ancient Greece? Day one, you arrive in Ancient Greece carrying flour, sugar, and an iron pot. Greek markets smell like fish and dog shit. These guys only eat plain bread. You prepare the dough and roll it up into small rings and drop them into hot oil. The dough starts to bubble and turn golden. You cover them with your thick, fluid, colorful toppings. People gather around and stare at you. Day two, you set up near a landmark. People line up at your stall. They call them Rings of the Gods. A man takes a bite, he almost faints with happiness. He tells you to put the donuts in the bag. He takes home 20 by sunset. The sugar rush kicks in. The city doesn't sleep. Day three, the city is addicted. People walk around holding bags of donuts. Philosophers start debating with frosting all over their robes. The demand doesn't stop. Gladiators chase the sugar rush before training. Week one, you're minding your business frying donuts. Fucking Socrates comes to rage bait you. He asks, does the donut control the man or does the man control the donut? You want to throw the fucking oil at him, but at this point you give up. You just hand him his donuts. He calls you a good boy and walks away happy this time he won. Month three, the lines are bigger than Socrates' head. You send a proposal to the emperor for a bigger shop. The emperor takes one bite and immediately builds you a Dunkin' Donuts. Now you don't have to lift a finger. Workers do everything for you. Donuts become a Greek obsession. Year one, Athens is now the donut capital. You've achieved a donut empire. Greece no longer runs on plain bread. It runs on sugar and thick white sauce. You didn't just bring donuts, you changed Greek culture.

### Structure:
- Day 1 → Day 2 → Day 3 → Week 1 → Month 3 → Year 1
- Time jumps accelerate (days → weeks → months → year)
- Each section escalates the absurdity

### Humor Style:
- Crude/vulgar ("smell like fish and dog shit", "thick white sauce")
- Socrates as recurring annoying character (rage bait philosopher)
- Modern franchise dropped into ancient world
- Exaggerated reactions (faints from happiness, city doesn't sleep)
- Swearing natural and frequent ("fucking Socrates")
- Punchline: "You didn't just bring donuts, you changed Greek culture"

You didn't just bring pancakes to Greece, you brought belly fat.

## KEY PATTERNS ACROSS ALL 3 VIDEOS

### Script Formula:
1. **Hook:** "What if you [verb] [modern thing] in Ancient Greece?" (0-2 seconds)
2. **Day 1:** Arrival + setup + first reactions from Greeks
3. **Day 2-3:** Escalation + people go crazy for it
4. **Week 1:** Socrates encounter (MANDATORY — he's the recurring antagonist)
5. **Month+:** Empire built / massive cultural impact
6. **Final line:** Punchline that mirrors the hook ("You didn't just bring X, you brought Y")

### Timing:
- 72-112 seconds (sweet spot ~90 seconds)
- ~150-250 words
- Time jumps accelerate: Days → Weeks → Months → Year

### Socrates Rules:
- He ALWAYS appears
- He's annoying, philosophical, rage-bait
- He asks dumb deep questions about the thing
- The protagonist gets frustrated with him
- He either gets owned (honey on face) or wins the interaction
- He's the comic relief villain

### Humor Mechanics:
- Modern thing in ancient setting = instant comedy
- Exaggerated Greek reactions (fainting, addiction, obsession)
- Vulgar/crude language lands well ("dog shit", "fucking Socrates")
- Deadpan delivery of absurd situations
- Short punchy sentences, not long descriptions
- Dark humor endings (diabetes, belly fat)
- The punchline ALWAYS calls back to the premise

### Narration Style:
- Second person "you" OR third person "he" — both work
- Fast-paced, continuous flow
- No dramatic pauses or mood changes
- Energy comes from the writing, not from delivery changes
- Sensory details for the product (bubbles forming, turning golden, honey dripping)

### Visual Style:
- Hyperrealistic AI-generated images (Midjourney/DALL-E quality)
- Glossy white skeleton with cartoonish round eyeballs
- Semi-transparent skin membrane over bones
- Ancient Greece setting (Parthenon, agora, marble streets)
- Warm golden Mediterranean lighting
- 9:16 vertical format
- Bold white captions with black outlines
- Skeleton wears anachronistic modern items (branded uniforms, etc.)
- Each scene = new AI-generated image with subtle motion (Kling animation)
